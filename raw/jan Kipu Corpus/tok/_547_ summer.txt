 tenpo seli lili lon ma Masatuse i

Marie Elliott pona a! kasi kule li pona lukin   tenpo lili la mi ike pilin tan ni: ma kasi li moli lukin lili. taso tenpo ni la mi sona sin e ni: ni li tenpo lete lon ma Masatuse, anu seme? a a a. ma mi la tenpo lete la kasi li weka ala e lipu. kasi pi mute lili taso li weka e lipu. sitelen ni li sitelen pi ma kasi pi tenpo lete. (mi ala li kama jo e sitelen ni.)

Serge Ganachaud toki pona sina li pona tawa mi tan ni: mi ken sona e ali. pakala wan taso li lon: o sitelen e nimi 'pilin ike'. nimi 'ike pilin' li nasin Inli. 
Like 路 Reply 路 5 hrs

Marie Elliott ni li pona mute tawa mi: sina sona pona e toki mi  jan li ken sitelen e ijo mute kepeken toki pona, anu seme? taso a, nimi "pilin ike" li nasin pi toki Inli. jan li pilin ike la pilin jan li pali ike. jan ni li kute ike anu lukin ike e ijo. anu jan li pilin ike e pilin ona. taso ken la pilin pi jan ni li pona. ken la jan ni li pilin lili e pilin pona ona. mi sona e ni: jan mute pi toki pona li sitelen e nimi "pilin ike". taso nimi "pona pilin" anu "ike pilin" li pona tawa mi.

Serge Ganachaud 
I like your toki pona because I can understand it all.  There is just one mistake.  Write `pilin ike'.  `Ike pilin' is English style.

Marie Elliott
I am very glad you understand my language well.  Can't one write many things in toki pona?
But, oh! `pilin ike' is English style.  If one feels badly, one's feelings work badly.  One hears things badly and sees things badly.  Or one things his thoughts badly.  But maybe this persons feelings are good.  Maybe this person thinks little hs good thoughts.  I know that many tokiponists write `pilin ike', but I like `pona pilin' and `ike pilin' ]
