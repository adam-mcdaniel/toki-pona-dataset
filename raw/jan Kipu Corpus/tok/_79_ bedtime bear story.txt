  jan lili pi kulupu mama mi li toki e nimi "o toki e toki musi kepeken toki pona. sina pu li ken toki pona. ni pini la mi mute li kama lape." tan ni la mi kama lukin e nimi ale li kulupu e ona lon ni:"soweli monsuta pimeja en meli mama ona li awen lon ma pi kasi suli. ona tu li moku e soweli lili pi tan lupa ma e kala pi tan telo linja e waso pi tan sewi e kili pi tan kasi lili. soweli monsuta li alasa e moku ni li kama sin lon tomo ona poka ona. ni pini la soweli mama li open e moku ali ni kepeken palisa luka ona. tenpo pi kon lete la soweli tu li wile moku e akesi e pipi kin tan ni: ona li ken ala lukin e ante lon ma walo. taso tenpo ni la nasin la ona tu li lape. taso tenpo mun pi kama pi lipu pi laso jelo la soweli mije li wile unpa li tawa tan tomo en tawa insa pi ma pi kasi suli tawa ni: ona li kama lukin e soweli monsuta meli. ona li kalama wawa e mu tawa ni: soweli meli li kute e ona li kama sona e wile ona. kin la wan li pali e ni. ona li pona lukin ni: ona li jo e linja sijelo loje e oko pimeja e monsi suwi. soweli mije li kama lon noka ona taso li pana e luka tawa sewi pi lawa ona li kipisi e selo pi kasi suli kepeken palisa luka ona lon sinpin pi meli. taso soweli mije ante li pali e sama. soweli tu li utala. soweli ante li uta e nena pi soweli mi li pali e sitelen lon ona. soweli mi li pilin ike li kama nasa li anpa e soweli ante li weka e ona. soweli meli li wile olin e mije ni. tan ni la ona tu li tawa lupa lon insa pi kiwen mute. tenpo seli la soweli meli li suli li supa lon ko lete li weka tan suno. tenpo mun pi lipu kule la meli li pana e soweli lili. ni la ona ali li awen pona"jan lili wan li toki e nimi "nanpa seme li nanpa e soweli lili?"-. "tu taso". jan lili ante li toki e nimi "soweli ante li moli anu seme?"-. "a! moli. selo ona li kama len namako pi poki suli. jan li esun e ona tawa mani mute." jan lili pi sona pilin li toki e nim' "soweli monsuta li pali ala pali e ijo jaki lon ma pi kasi suli?"-. "pali li kepeken jan lili pi sona pilin tawa ilo pi pona monsi. tenpo ni la o tawa supa lape."

[A chlld in my family said "Tell us an amusing tale in toki pona.  You study the textbook and talk well. After that, we will go to sleep."  So I found all the words and gathered them in this:

"A bear and his mother lived in the woods.  The two ate varmits from holes in the ground and fish from the river and birds from the air and fruit from shrubs.  The bear hunted this food and came back home with it.  After that the mother bear opened all the food with her claws.  In the winter, the two bears had to eat lizards and even bugs because they could not see other things in the white world.  But then usually the two slept.  But in the moon of the arrival of green leaves, the bear was horny and went from home and went into the woods to find a female bear.  He sounded a mighty roar so that a feamle bear would hear it and learn of his desire. Indeed, one did this.  She was so good looking: she had red fur and dark eyes and a cute rump.  The bear came up on only his hind legs and threw his forelegs above his head and cut bark of a tree with his claws in front of female.  But another male bear did the same.  The two bears fought.  The other bear bit the snout of my bear and made a mark on it.  My bear was angry and went crazy and defeated the other bear and sent him away. The female had to love this male.  So the two went to a hole in the midst of many rocks.  In summer the female bear was big and lay on the cool sand and stayed out of the sun.  In the month of colored leaves, when the grain was ripe, the female birthed cubs.  In this case, everyone was fine." 

One child aked "How many cubs were there?"  "Just two."  Another chlld asked "Was the other bear dead?"  "Oh, dead.  His hide became a fancy lining for a trunk. A man traded it for a lot of money."  A wise-guy child asked "Did the bear shit in the woods?"  "Yes, and uses wise-guy kids to wipe his butt.  Now go to bed."
