[I wrote her name
On the river side. But,
Oh, the water flowed and erased
And felt an evil.  White ground.
She said "You
Are bad and foolish.  I will not
Exist. My name is similarly mortal.
When I die, I am dust and air."
I said "No, no!
You are different because of my writings.
If my texts endure, 
Your fame will be high.
I death defeats everything,
Our love will be everything indeed.

AMORETTI, SONNET #75
By Edmund Spenser
One day I wrote her name upon the strand,
But came the waves and washed it away:
Again I write it with a second hand,
But came the tide, and made my pains his prey.
Vain man, said she, that doest in vain assay,
A mortal thing so to immortalize,
For I myself shall like to this decay,
And eek my name be wiped out likewise.
Not so, (quod I) let baser things devise
To die in dust, but you shall live by fame:
My verse, your virtues rare shall eternize,
And in the heavens write your glorious name.
Where whenas death shall all the world subdue,
Our love shall live, and later life renew.]
