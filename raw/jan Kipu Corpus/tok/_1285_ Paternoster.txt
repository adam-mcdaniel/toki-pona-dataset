  'sewi en pona' is questionable as a mixed predicate since they seem to be in different categories, prob 'sewi li pona', tp doesn't use the 'en ... en ...' construction for parallels, just the 'en' bbetween the components (or maybe 'sama'). prepositional phrases go after the direct object, so 'pana e moku tawa mi lon tenpo suno ni'. '... 'ike mi sama ni: mi weka ...' 'awen weka e mi' 'ni li nasin' is interesting for "amen" (see discussion a couple of days ago).
