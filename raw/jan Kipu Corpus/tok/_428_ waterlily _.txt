toki pi kasi sike
meli Naja li jan pi ma kasi Amason. ona li kute e toki ni: mun li olin e meli la ona li ante e meli ni tawa mun. mije utala mute li olin e meli Naja. taso ona li wile e olin mun taso.
tenpo pimeja la ona li lukin e mun kepeken olin suli. mun li tawa la ona li tawa sama. suno sin kin li kama la ona li tawa mun kepeken wawa ona ali. tenpo pimeja ale la ona li awen sama. sijelo pi meli Naja li kama ike tan ni. taso ona li pini ala olin e mun.
ni li kama: ona li kama wawa ala li lape lon poka telo. sitelen pi sama mun li kama lon telo. meli Naja li sewi li lukin e sitelen ni li pilin e ni: mun li kama tawa ona! tan ni la ona li tawa telo. taso pini ni la jan li ken ala lukin e ona. ona li moli lon telo.
mun li pilin ike tan moli pi meli Naja. mun li pana e pona tawa ona li ante e ona tawa mun. taso mun ni li ante tan mun pi lon sewi. ona li lon telo li kasi sike li jo e kasi kule pi kon pona. kasi kule ni li open lon tenpo pi kama mun taso.
pini! 
toki ni li tan toki kulupu Pasila. Tp Antônio Lucas Araújo Moreira

[Tale of the water lily.
Naja was a woman of the Amazon jungle.  She ehard the tale that, if the moon loved a woman, he changed her into a moon.  Many warriors loved Naja, but she wanted only the love of the moon.
At night she looked at the moon with great love.  As teh moon moved, she moved the same way.  Even at sunrise, she went to the moon with all her strength.  Every night she continued the same,  Naja's body became very ill because of this.  But she did ot stop loving the moon.
It happened that she became weak and slept beside the water.  And image like the moon appeared on the water. Naja got up and saw the image and thought that the moon had come for her.  So she went to the water.  But in the end, people could not find her.  She was dead in the water.
The moon felt bad about the death of Naja.  The moon blessed her and changed her into a moon.  But this moon was different from the moon in the sky.  This was in the water and was a round plant and had a pleasant smelling flower.  This flower opens only at moonrise.
End.
A tale from tribes in Brazil.]
