[my rough translation:
Me and my family travelled to Tennessee. When our car crossed the border, the stars were hot. The moon was burning. It was huge and above and burning. The bodies of animals slept in the road. Tennessee waited. Don't stay in Tennessee. Tennessee watches. It is the eyes of something huge. It watches your soul. Tennessee is here. 
We stopped the car outside a motel. My parents sorted the stuff in the trunk of the car, so I made my mind up to look around the place. Its walls were made of wood, but they were shifting a little. I put my hand in the wall. It was like a baby's breathing. In. Out. In. Out. But Tennessee is still. 
That night, I didn't sleep. The moon was huge outside my window. It was inhuman, but it seemed to be watching me. This whole country was looking at me.  It was staring at me. My parents slept but didn't breathe. My breaths were ragged. The walls shifted a little, in and out and in and out. The moon watched. The land watched. Tennessee is still. Do not stay, traveller. Only Tennessee can remain here. You cannot.]
