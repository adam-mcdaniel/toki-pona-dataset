  John Clifford This is somewhat new ground, so the comments here are not critical but merely comparative with the norms in other contexts.Stephen Waldron (jan Alija en jan Elena li lon tomo sona ona) jan Alija: meli o, sona tawa li kiwen. a ike. 'kiwen' is hard like stone or metal, not like calculus or even mountain climbing, "difficult". The old lists offer 'pi pali mute', 'pona ala', and 'wawa' and I think someone suggested 'ike pali' more recently (we need to get those lists updated). I'm used to seeing 'ike a' but I don't know what the difference is, if any, or how fixed the habit is. 'sona tawa' "moving knowledge"? "science of motion" I wouldn't expect to understand today's students nor for them to use stadard terms.jan Elena: sona! mije Sipo li pana e sona ike. ona li toki li toki li toki. aa mi ken ala. ona li musi ala.
