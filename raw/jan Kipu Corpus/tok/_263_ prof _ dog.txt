jan sona en soweli pona

tenpo open la jan sona li jo e soweli pona.
[`soweli pona' = dog? cat?  Settle for "pet"]
jan sona li pali lon tomo sona, tenpo sama la soweli pona li awen lon tomo pi jan sona.
tomo sona la jan lili li kute e toki pi jan sona.
jan sona la "mi sona suli, sina sona lili, mi ken pana e sona tawa sina."
[This form for dialog is not really standard but seems appropriate and handy.]
[Prob. `sona e suli' etc., to fit with the end and the general pattern.]
jan lili la "sin la mi ken ala ken pana e sona tawa sina?"
[`sin'? Maybe `kin' "however"?]
jan sona la "ala! mi sona e sona sina ali."
jan sona li toki mute.
...
tenpo wan la soweli pona li tawa lon tomo sona.
[no `lon' or else `kama ` for `tawa']
"a! pona!" jan lili li toki e ni, "soweli li pona, a! mi musi, a!"
jan sona la "sina musi tan soweli pona, taso sina sona ala tan soweli pona."
[`tan'? `kepeken' seems safer.]
jan lili la "tenpo ni la mi ken ala ken musi tan soweli pona?"
jan sona la "ala! tomo ni li tomo musi ala, tomo ni li tomo sona."
jan lili la "soweli li ken ala ken pana sona e mi?"
jan sona la "ala! soweli li jo e sona ala, soweli li ken ala pana e sona tawa sina."
jan sona li weka e soweli pona tan tomo sona, jan lili li pini musi.
jan sona li toki mute sin.
...
tenpo pi pini toki la jan lili li tawa lon selo pi tomo sona li lukin e soweli pona.
[see above on `tawa lon']
soweli pona li mu kalama li tawa wawa.
jan lili li tawa lon monsi pi nasin soweli.
[not sure the `nasin' is needed "at the back of the critter's trail" seems odd for "behind the critter "`lon monsi soweli']
soweli pona li tawa lon tomo moku sin, jan lili li musi.
[see above on `tawa lon' .  Why `sin'?-- none mentioned before.]
soweli li moku mute, jan lili li moku mute.
...
tomo sona la jan lili li sewi e luka sina.
jan sona la "jan lili o, sina wile ala wile e toki?"
[He doesn't want a talk, he wants to talk, just `wile toki']
jan lili la "wile! tenpo moku sin li lon seme?"
[Why `sin'?  This seems convoluted, but probably works ok.]
jan sona la "mi sona ala, ni la tenpo moku sin li lon ala."
[convoluted again]
jan lili la "ala! mi sona, a!"
jan sona la "ken ala! mi sona e sona sina ali."
jan lili la "ala! soweli pona li pana e sona ni tawa mi, a!"
jan sona la "ken ala! mi sona suli, sina sona lili, soweli li sona ala."
jan lili li toki wawa, taso jan sona li weka e toki pi jan lili.
jan sona li toki mute.
...
tenpo pini la soweli li moku mute, jan sona [prob `lili'] li moku mute, taso jan sona li moku lili.

[The educated man and his pet

An educated man had a pet.
The educated man worked at a school.  At the same time, his pet stayed at the educated man's house. 
At school, children heard the talk of the educated man.
He said "I know a lot.  You know a little.  I can give you knowledge."
A  child said "However, can I teach you?"
Educated man: "No!  I know everything you know."
The educated man talked a lot.

One time the pet came to the school.
The children said "Oh, goody! Hey!  The animal is nice.  We will have fun!"
Educated man: "You have fun with the pet, but you don't know with the pet."
Children: "Can we play with the pet now?"
Educated man: "No!  This place is not a playroom; it is a school."
Children: "Can an animal teach us?"
Educated man: "No! Animals have no knowledge.  Animals can not teach you."
The educated man removed the pet from the school.  The children stopped having fun.
The educated man talked a lot more.  

When the talking ended, the children went out of the school and saw the pet.  
The pet sounded loudly and ran. 
The children followed the pet.
 The pet went to a new restaurant.  The children had fun.
The animal ate a lot.  The children ate a lot.

At school, a child raised his hand.
Educated man: "Child, do you want to speak?"
Child: "Yes. When is the new meal time?'
Educated man:  "I don't know.  Here there isn't a new meal time."
Child: "No!  I know indeed."
Educated man "Impossible.  I know everything you know."
Child: "No. the pet taught me this, in fact."
Educated man: "Impossible.  I know a lot.  You know a little.  Animals know nothing.'
The child talked loudly but the educated man disregarded the child's remarks.
The educated man talked a lot.

In the end, the pet ate a lot.  The children ate a lot.  But the educated man ate little]
