sitelen pona pi jan Sonja la sitelen 'pona' li pona tawa mi.tawa lukin la sitelen 'pona' li ni: pini tu uta li tawa sewi.sitelen 'ike' la pini tu uta li tawa anpa.ni li pona tan seme?ijo li pona tawa mi la pini tu pi uta mi li tawa sewi.ijo li ike tawa mi la pini tu pi uta mi li tawa anpa.ijo li pona la mi ken pilin pona tan ona.ijo li ike la mi ken pilin ike tan ona.o kute e pilin sina.o sona e pilin sina.o ken e ni: pilin sina li pana e sona tawa lawa sina.pilin li suli.


[Joshua Birns-Sprague

In Sonja's hieroglyphs, I like the sign `pona'.
To look at, the sign `pona' is the two ends of a mouth going upward.
In the sign `ike', the two ends go downward.
Why is this good?
If I like something, the two ends of my mouth go upward.
If I dislike something, the two ends of my mouth go downward.
 If something is good, I can feel good because of it.
If something is bad, I can feel bad because of it.
Listen to your feelings.
Know your feelings.
Allow your feelings to give knowledge to your head.
Feelings are important.]
