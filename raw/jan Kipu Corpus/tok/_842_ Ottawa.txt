`mi ken ala toki lon kulupu ni pi tenpo mute'
Prob `lon tenpo mute' "on many occasionas, often" rather than "this frequent group"

`mi lon tomo mi sin la, mi toki tawa sina ali sin'
Presumably `sin' in the sense of "again", not "new", `lon sin' and `toki sin' are clearer at least (and probably more).  

`o toki tawa mi mute e wawa lete ona.'
Prepositional phrases after direct object (the other way seems to introduce even more ambiguities but needs more research)
Presumably "tell me about", so `toki e ijo pi wawa lete ona'
Not sure about the end: "the strength of its cold" `wawa pi lete ona'  or "its strong cold" `lete wawa ona' both seem likely. 

`o jo e tenpo pona lon ni!'
Not something you can command exactly, but something you can wish.  English regularly screws this up (I don't know about French) but that is no excuse for tp.  `o sina jo e tenpo ponaS lon ni'
