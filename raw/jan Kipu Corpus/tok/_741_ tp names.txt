So you want to make a toki pona name for yourself.  The easy way to do this is as follows: find a word you like , such that you would be content if people called by that word.  Take it and change it to toki pona using the method below. I will use the word "Studmuffin".\

Change the consonants accoring to this table
b becomes p
c becomes k or s, depending on its sound  (similarly, ch)
d becomes t
f bcomes p
g becomes k or s
h disappears
j becomes s or (if you want) j (for Spanish, if you want, k)
k remains k
l remains l
m remains m (but at the end becomes n)
n stays n (ng, too)
p remains p (ph, too)
r becomes l or w (in American words) or k (in French words)
s remains s (sh, too)
t stays t (but some people use s for th, some don't)
v becomes p or w (your choice)
w remains w (also wh)
x becomes s (maybe k).
y becomes j
z becomes s

After this you have 'stutmuppin'.  This is not a word in the scope of toki pona.  Consonants are next to each other.  Syllables end in consonants that are not n. And it sounds weird. "stootmooppeen".  

You can use two methods to get rid of syllable final consonants other than n.  1.  You can stick a vowel in after it. You can use any vowel you like.  2.  You can discard the consonant.  Using the first method, you get 'stutimupipin' (or with other vowels).  Using the second method, you get 'stumupin'.  I think the second word is much better than the first, so proceed using the second word..

If two consonants come together not at the end of a syllable, you do the same thing: you can stick a vowel between them or you can drop one of them.  This gives ''situmupin' (or with a different vowel) or 'sumupin' or 'tumupin'.  I like the third word, but you may disagree. 

But this word still sound weird, "toomoopeen"  Unfortunately, English has fifteen vowels, so ten of them are not in toki pona.  All the vowels in "Studmuffin"  are in that group.  So you can use one sound and use another. Finally, you find a vowel you like.  I use 'samapen' . But that is just me.  If you want something else, that is right  too. 

You can use this method with any word.  Especially, you can use it with your family name, with your first name or your last name or your middle name or your nick name.  The process is the same.  So make your own name for yourself.  

Now you have a proper name, an Unofficial Word. You need to know two thing about unofficial words . 1. Unofficial Words bergin with a capital letter.  Indeed, only Unofficial Words begin with a capital letter.  So the word above should be "Samapen"

2. Unoffficial Words are adjectives.  So if one can occur in a sentence, a noun must be in front of it.  If you want to talk about John, you have to use 'jan San' and not just 'San'.

*'pilin pona' is a terrible expression, but everybody uses it, so it stays.
