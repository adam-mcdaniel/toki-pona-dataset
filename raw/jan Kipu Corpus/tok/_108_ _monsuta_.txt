monsuta
nimi "monsuta" li ike tawa jan mute. taso ona li pona tawa mi tan ni: nimi "monsuta" li ken e ni: sina toki e pilin "fear". nimi monsuta li nimi "threat" kepeken toki Inli. sina wile toki e nimi "I am afraid", la sina ken toki e nimi "mi pilin e monsuta". ni li nimi "I think about a threat" kepeken toki Inli. nasin sin li lon ala. tan ni la nimi "monsuta" li pona tawa mi.
[Stephen Waldron]

108a

monsuta
nimi "monsuta" li ike tawa jan mute. taso ona li pona tawa mi tan ni: nimi "monsuta" li ken e ni: sina toki e ijo pi pilin "fear". nimi `monsuta' li nimi "threat" kepeken toki Inli. sina wile toki e nimi "I am afraid", la sina ken toki e nimi "mi pilin e monsuta". ni li nimi "I think about a threat" kepeken toki Inli. nasin sin li lon ala. tan ni la nimi "monsuta" li pona tawa mi

[Many people dislike `monsuta'.  But I like it because `monsuta' allows you to talk about the idea "fear". 
`monsuta' is "threat" in English. If you want to say "I am afraid", you can say `mi pilin e monsuta'.  This is "I think about a threat" in English.  This new method is not right (?). So, I like `monsuta'.]
[This reflects an earlier stage in the development of `monsuta' -- and of "thinking about".]
