  ni la mi tu e ante toki pi jan ante. taso mi kepeken nasin ante lon nimi pi tenpo mun.
  "telo sewi pi tenpo mun Apili li kama e kasi kule pi tenpo mun Maju."
  [Joshua Birns-Sprague]
   ma Kanse la, jan li toki e ni: "tenpo mun Apili la, o weka ala e len lili kin. tenpo mun Maju la, o pali e wile sina."
   [Serge Ganachaud]
   mi sona e nimi ni! lon ma tomo mi la telo li kama kin tan sewi. mi wile lukin e kasi kule!
      [Sarah Brown]

[In this case, I copied another person's translation.  But I used a different method for the names of months.  "The rain of April brings the flowers of May". 
In French, one says "In april, don't throw away your underwear.  But in May, do what you want."
I know this expression.  In my city, water indeed comes from the sky.  I want to see the flowers.]
