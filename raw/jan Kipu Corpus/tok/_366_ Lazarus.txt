meli wawa li jo e ilo suno la

MAMA PI JAN WEKA. luka suno ona li pana
kama pona tawa ma ale. oko ona li pana e lawa tawa
tomo pi tomo tawa telo insa pi ma tomo tu.
kepeken uta pi kalama ala la ona li toki e ni:
ma majuna o awen e wawa sina!
o pana tawa mi jan sina pi wawa ala e jan pi mani ala
jan mute sina li wile kama jo e kon pona
jan sina pi pilin jaki lon poka telo ona.
o pana e jan ni pi tomo ala tawa mi!
lon poka lupa wawa la mi sewi e ilo suno mi!

Unlike the big metal man of Greece
Who stood with mighty legs on two lands, 
On your sunset shore,
A might woman holds a lamp 
Of intense brightness and has the name
Mother of Discarded People.  Her shining hand sends
A welcome to all countries.  Her eyes command 
The harbor between two cities.
With silent mouth she says
"Old countries, keep your power.
Give your weak, and your poor,
And your many who want a free breath, 
And the wretched of your shores.
Give these homeless to me.
I raise my lamp beside the golden door."

The New Colossus
BY EMMA LAZARUS
  Not like the brazen giant of Greek fame,
  With conquering limbs astride from land to land;
  Here at our sea-washed, sunset gates shall stand
  A mighty woman with a torch, whose flame
  Is the imprisoned lightning, and her name
  Mother of Exiles. From her beacon-hand
  Glows world-wide welcome; her mild eyes command
  The air-bridged harbor that twin cities frame.
  "Keep, ancient lands, your storied pomp!" cries she
  With silent lips. "Give me your tired, your poor,
  Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free,
  The wretched refuse of your teeming shore.
  Send these, the homeless, tempest-tost to me,
  I lift my lamp beside the golden door!"
