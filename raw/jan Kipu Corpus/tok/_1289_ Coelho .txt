mi ante toki e toki musi lili sin pi jan Palo Kolo tawa toki pona. lukin pona. 

jan pi sona sewi li wile e ni: jan pi kama sona li awen e ma pan.
tenpo sike nanpa wan la jan pi kama sona li pana e telo tawa ma pan lon tenpo wile ale. pan li kama wawa li mute pona.
tenpo sike nanpa tu la ona li pana e telo e moku ma. pan li kama mute suli lon tenpo lili.
tenpo sike nanpa tu wan la ona li pana e moku ma mute. pan li kama mute suli mute. taso pan li wawa ala.
jan pi sona sewi li toki e ni: "sina awen pana e moku ma mute la sina jo e pan ike lon tenpo sike kama. sina pali lili e pona tawa jan la sina wawa e ona. sina pali suli e pona tawa jan la sina wawa ala e ona."

[Joshua Birns-Sprague‎t

I translated a new little anecdote by Paul Coelho to toki pona.  Enjoy reading!
A spiritual master wants a student to keep a grain field. 
In the first year, the student brings water to the grain field whenever it is needed.  The grain becomes strong and multiplies well.
The second year, he brings water and fertilizer.  The grain multiplies a lot quickly.
The third year, he brings a lot of fertilizer.  The grain multiplies very greatly.  But the grain is weak.
The spiritual master says "If you continue to give a lot of fertilizer, you get worse grain in the coming years.  If you do a little good for a person, you strengthen him.  If you do a lot of good for a person, you weaken him."]
