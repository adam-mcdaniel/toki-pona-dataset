tenpo ijo la jan Pipo Pakin pi tomo pini-poki li toki e ni: tenpo kama lili la ona li wile kama musi tan ni: tenpo nanpa ale luka luka wan la, ona li sike e suno. tan kama suli ni la jan ma sama li toki mute e ona li musi kin tan ona.
jan Pipo li jo e mani mute li nasa lukin. tenpo pi mute mute mute la jan ma li musi tan jan Pipo. ona li kama tan ni: tenpo pini la jan Pipo li tawa weka. taso ona li kama tawa tomo lon tenpo kama lili. jan ale li pilin e weka ona. taso jan ala li awen e kama ona. ona li tawa tomo, la li kama jo e mani mute. tan ni la jan Pipo li kama suli lon ma tomo. jan ale li toki e jan ni li pilin e ni: nena pi lon tomo pini-poki li jo e mani suli mute. toki ona li suli kin ala, la jan Pipo li jo e sijelo pona. musi la ni li kama: tenpo li kama. taso tenpo li pakala ala e jan Pipo. tenpo ale la ona li sama lukin. tenpo lili la jan li toki e ni: ona li wawa pona. taso ken la toki pi pona mute li ni: ona li wawa pona ala li ante ala kin sijelo. jan pi nanpa lili li pilin ike tan kama ni. ona ni li pilin e ni: wawa pi lon tenpo en kin mani mute li ike tawa jan ante. ona li ken ala pona.
jan li toki e ni: "tenpo kama la ona li wile kama jo e ike. ni li pona ala. ike li kama tan ni a!"

  taso tenpo ni la ike li kama ala. kin la jan Pipo li pona kepeken mani ona. tan ni la jan mute li pilin e ni: nasa lili la ona li pona li jo e kama pona. kulupu mama ona li ken tawa tomo ona (taso kin kulupu Tomopoki-Pakin li ken ala). jan mute pi sama ni li olin e jan Pipo: li jo e mani lili e kulupu mama pi wawa ala pi lon ma tomo. taso tenpo suli la jan Pipo li poka ala pi jan pona. tenpo pini lili taso, la mama sama lili li kama suli.
  mama sama ijo li jan Poto Pakin. tenpo lili la mama sama ante li sike e suno. tenpo mute la jan Poto li sike e ona. tenpo pini lili la jan Pipo li wile e ni: jan Pipo li lon ala kin lon tenpo kama, la li pana e mani tawa jan Poto. kulupu Tomopoki-Pakin, la wile ona li pakala kin. kin la ni li kama: jan Pipo en jan Poto li sike e suno lon tenpo sama, tenpo mun luka tu tu lon tenpo suno mute mute tu. "sina o wile kama tawa tomo poka mi, jan Poto suwi o." tenpo ijo la jan Pipo toki e ona. Once upon a time, Bilbo Baggins of Bagend said that in a little while he wanted to throw a party because it was his 111th birthday. Because of this great event, the people of his country talked a lot about him and even had fun because of him.
Bilbo had a lot of money and was strange looking. Very very often, the country folk were amused because of Bilbo.  This came about because once Bilbo went away.  But he came home a little later.  Everybody thought about his going away.  But nobody remembered his return.  He, when he came home, had a lot of money.  So, Bilbo became important in the town.  Everybody talked about this person and thought that the hill of Bagend has very large heaps of money. When there talk was not to great, Bilbo had his health.  Amusingly, this happened: time passed. But time did not harm Bilbo.  He always looked the same.  Occasionally, people said he was good and strong.  But maybe the talk of many good folks was that he wasn't good and strong but that he really changed his body.  A few people felt bad because of this situation.  They thought that the power in time and much money too was bad for other people.  This could not be good.  People said "He will want to get evil.  This is not good.  Evil will come because of this."
  But so far  no evil had happened.  Indeed, Bilbo did good with his money.  So most people thought that, somewhat strangely, he was good and had good luck.  His family could go to his home (but, however, the clan House-end Baggins could not.) Most people like this loved Bilbo: they had a little money abd a poor family in the town.  But for a long time, Bilbo was not near friends.  Only a little while before, his nephew came of age.
  This nephew was Frodo Baggins.  Frodo was older than another nephew.  A little earlier, Bilbo wished that Bilbo, if he were not still here in the future, wuld give the money to Frodo.  According to the House-end Baggins, his desire was very painful.  
  Moreover, it happened that Bilbo and Frodo had the same birthday, the forty second day of the ninth month. "Dear Frodo, please come home with me"  Bilbo once said this.  "Because we can throw a birthday party."  At that time, Frodo was "slightly many" years old.  People say "slightly many" from forty to sixty three.
