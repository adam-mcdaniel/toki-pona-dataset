 The old man and the sea by Hemingway
 There was a man. He was very old.  Alone he caught fish from the Gulf Stream and sailed a little boat.  For many months, he caught no fish. For a little while, a child stayed with the old man.  But for a long time they two did not have fish.  So, his parents told the child that this old man was very crazy and like `salajao'.  The word `salajo' means that often many bad things or not good things happen to a person.  The child, when he heard his parents, went to another boat. In a few days, the men in this boat got three good fish.  The child saw the old man and felt bad because every day the old man came home from his boat and did not have a fish. Te child always went to the old man and helped him by carrying the grar from the boat.  This gear was lines and a gaff and a harpoon and a sail.  This sail was a little torn and was wrapped around the mast of the boat.  The look of the sail seemed to say things always defeat the man.  ]
